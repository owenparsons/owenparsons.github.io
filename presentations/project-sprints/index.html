Project sprints

What we did and what we learned

What is a 'project sprint'?

  • Short research projects (SRPs)
  • Intended to fill gaps between paid work
  • Ideally aligned with long term product goals

What was good?

  • Gave people a chance to suggest ideas and work on things that excite them
  • No pressure from customers
  • Utilised downtime
  • Explored R&D directions that led to projects

What could have been improved?

  • Process to clearly prioritise project ideas
  • Input from commerical and more strategically involved people on project directions
  • Impact tracking (longer-term timeframes)
  • Clear framework for managing non-paid work

Improving the process

  • Choosing areas of focus
  • Calibrating effort
  • Tracking impact

Choosing areas of focus

  • Need to scope and priortise projects so they align with company focus
    • Upskilling people in areas we want to focus on
    • Creating demos and prototypes
    • Assessing feasibility of long term projects
  • Guiding focus should come from wider team
    • Collaborative approaches to idea submission seems to work but priortitisation needs more centralised input
    • Commercial team can track requests from prospective customers to give signal on areas of focus
    • Strategic teams should be involved in prioritisation

Calibrating effort

  • How much time/effort should be spent on non-paid work?
  • Is there a minimum threshold below which non-paid work doesn't return benefits.
  • At what point does non-paid work become a distraction from paid work?

Tracking impact

  • Fundamentally, we are a business and we need to make money.
  • Important to find ways to measure impact of non-paid work.
  • This can help us to calibrate in two main ways:
    • What should we be focusing on?
    • How much resource should we be allocating to non-paid work?

TL;DR

  • Choosing what to focus on in should be driven by key members across the company.
  • The could be a lot of benefits from having clear processes for prioritisation of unpair work.
  • Quantifying the impact of non-paid work will be challenging but is important to help us calibrate and make better decisions.
